Ian Parberry

I received a BS with First Class Honours in Computer Science and Mathematics in 1981 from the University of Queensland and a PhD in Computer Science in 1984 from the University of Warwick. After Assistant Professoring at Penn State I joined the faculty of the Department of Computer Sciences of the University of North Texas at the rank of Associate Professor in 1990. I retired as a Full Professor in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering in 2022. I pioneered the teaching of game programming to undergraduates in 1993 and served as Founding Director of the Laboratory for Recreational Computing until 2022. My research intersected many areas of computer science including entertainment computing, AI, computer graphics, neural networks, parallel computing, and theoretical computer science. I've been cited over 3500 times, my h-index is 30, my i10-index is 66, my Erdös number is 3, my Bacon Number is 3, and my Erdös-Bacon Number is 6.
